Storage Safety Considerations

Hydrogen storage requires careful attention to pressure management, material compatibility, and leak detection. Storage systems must be designed to withstand high pressures and prevent hydrogen embrittlement.

Key storage methods include:

  • Compressed Gas Storage: High-pressure tanks (350-700 bar) requiring robust materials
  • Liquid Hydrogen: Cryogenic storage at -253°C with specialized insulation
  • Metal Hydrides: Chemical storage in solid materials
  • Underground Storage: Large-scale storage in caverns or depleted gas fields

Personal Protective Equipment

Working with hydrogen requires appropriate PPE to protect against cold burns, pressure hazards, and fire risks:

  • Eye Protection: Safety glasses or face shields for splash protection
  • Hand Protection: Insulated gloves for handling cryogenic equipment
  • Body Protection: Fire-resistant clothing and thermal protection
  • Respiratory Protection: In confined spaces or during emergency response
  • Gas Detection: Portable hydrogen sensors for leak detection
